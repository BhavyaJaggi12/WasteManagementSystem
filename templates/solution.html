How We Address the Problem

We create a website that collects menstrual waste from households and recycles it in our waste management plant.

Segregation and Collection

  • Collect the Waste: Menstrual waste is collected from households using our dedicated collection system. The collected waste is then transported to our waste management plant.

Mechanical Drying to Remove Moisture

  • Drying Chambers: Industrial-scale drying equipment is used for the large-scale drying of pads. This equipment works by removing moisture using heat and airflow.

Gasification Process

  • Pyrolysis: The dried pads are heated in the absence of oxygen in a closed chamber called a gasifier. As the pads are heated, organic material disintegrates to form a gas mixture containing carbon monoxide, hydrogen, methane, and carbon dioxide.
  • Partial Oxidation: Oxygen is introduced into the gasifier in a process known as partial oxidation. This reacts with the carbon in the pads to produce more carbon monoxide and hydrogen.
  • Gas Cleaning: The resulting gases primarily consist of:
    • Carbon monoxide (CO)
    • Hydrogen (H2)
    • Carbon dioxide (CO2)
    • Methane (CH4)
    • Nitrogen (N2)
    • Other trace gases
    These gases are cleaned and compressed to remove impurities such as tar and particulate matter. This involves passing the gas through filters or scrubbers.

Gas Utilization

  • Combustion: The cleaned producer gas can be burned in a combustion engine to generate electricity or heat.
  • Syngas Production: The gas can also be further processed to produce syngas, a mixture of carbon monoxide and hydrogen. Syngas can be used as a feedstock for the production of fuels, chemicals, and other products.
